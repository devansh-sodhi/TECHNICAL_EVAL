# Agent Coaching Notes

## Sara — T-1041

### What Went Well

**Empathy and understanding of the customer's frustration.**
> "I completely understand your frustration, and I appreciate your patience."
*Sara acknowledges the customer's feelings, which helps to build rapport and shows that she is listening.*

**Clear communication of the situation and next steps.**
> "I can see the withdrawal was initiated on the 12th. It looks like it's been flagged for a manual compliance review, which can take up to 7 business days."
*Sara provides a clear explanation of the status of the withdrawal, which helps the customer understand the delay.*

### Development Areas

**Managing customer expectations regarding processing times.**
> "I can't guarantee same-day processing, but escalating will ensure it's reviewed as a priority — typically within 24 hours."
Suggested: *"Consider providing a more optimistic timeframe or additional reassurance about the escalation process."*
*While it's important to set realistic expectations, offering a more positive outlook could help alleviate some of the customer's frustration.*

**Closing the conversation with a more personalized touch.**
> "Great, thank you for your patience and for calling Deriv. Have a great day!"
Suggested: *"Add a closing statement that invites the customer to reach out again if they have further questions or concerns."*
*This would leave the customer feeling more valued and supported, encouraging them to return for future assistance.*

## Marcus — T-1042

### What Went Well

**Professional greeting and identification.**

*Marcus starts the call with a clear and professional introduction, setting a positive tone.*

**Acknowledging the customer's feelings.**
> "I get that you're annoyed but there's not much I can do."
*Marcus acknowledges the customer's frustration, which is important in customer service to show empathy.*

### Development Areas

**Providing more detailed explanations.**
> "It looks like there were some unusual login patterns."
Suggested: *"Explain what unusual login patterns mean and why they might trigger a suspension."*
*Providing more context can help the customer understand the situation better and reduce frustration.*

**Offering solutions or alternatives.**
> "I can log a note if you want."
Suggested: *"Offer to escalate the issue or provide a timeline for when the customer might hear back from the fraud team."*
*Offering a potential next step or escalation can help the customer feel more supported and less helpless.*

## Linda — T-1043

### What Went Well

**Clear and concise information delivery**
> "For UK clients under FCA regulation, the maximum leverage for major forex pairs is 1:30 for retail clients. If you're a professional client, that can go up to 1:500."
*Linda provided accurate and straightforward information regarding leverage limits, which is essential for the customer's understanding.*

**Polite and friendly closing**
> "My pleasure. Thanks for calling Deriv — have a lovely day!"
*Linda ended the call on a positive note, which helps in building rapport and leaves a good impression on the customer.*

### Development Areas

**Probing for additional needs**
> "Wonderful! Is there anything else I can help with today?"
Suggested: *"Consider asking more specific questions to uncover any additional needs, such as 'Are you interested in any other trading features or services?'"*
*This could help identify other areas where the customer might need assistance, enhancing their overall experience.*

**Providing additional context**
> "There's no minimum deposit for a standard account, though some trading platforms have their own minimums."
Suggested: *"You could elaborate on what those trading platforms are and their specific minimums to provide a fuller picture."*
*This would give the customer a more comprehensive understanding of the options available to them.*
